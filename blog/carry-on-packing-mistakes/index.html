10 things people always forget when packing carry-on

Travel · 6 min read

Packing carry-on only is freeing — no checked-bag fees, no waiting at the carousel, no lost luggage. But the smaller you pack, the more it stings to realise you forgot something essential. After thousands of trips' worth of feedback from travellers, the same items show up again and again on the "I forgot it" list.

The 10 most-forgotten essentials

  • Phone charger & cable — the single most forgotten item. Pack it first, not last.
  • Travel power adapter — your charger is useless abroad without the right plug.
  • Medications — prescriptions and basics like painkillers, antihistamines and motion-sickness tablets.
  • A spare set of underwear in your personal item — in case your bag is delayed or you get caught out.
  • Reusable water bottle — empty through security, fill after. Saves money and plastic.
  • Sunglasses — easy to leave on the kitchen table, annoying to buy on arrival.
  • A light layer — planes and evenings get cold even on summer trips.
  • Toothbrush & travel toiletries — under 100ml, in a clear bag.
  • Copies of key documents — passport, insurance, bookings (a photo on your phone is enough).
  • A small day bag — so you're not lugging your full pack around the city.

Why we forget — and how to fix it

We don't forget because we're careless; we forget because we pack from memory, late at night, under time pressure. The fix isn't "try harder" — it's to pack from a list instead of from memory. A good packing list does three things: it's tailored to your destination and trip length, it reminds you of the critical items before you leave, and it adapts to the activities you've planned (a beach trip and a ski trip need very different bags).

The other half of carry-on success is weight. Airlines enforce cabin limits more strictly than ever, and an overweight bag at the gate can cost more than a checked bag would have. Knowing your bag's weight before you leave home — and your specific airline's limit — turns a stressful gate moment into a non-event.

A 60-second packing routine

Open your list, lay everything out before you put it in the bag, tick items as they go in, then weigh the bag. If you're over, the heaviest "nice to have" comes out first. Do this once and the forgotten-charger panic becomes a thing of the past.
