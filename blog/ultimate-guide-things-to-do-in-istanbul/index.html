The Ultimate Guide to Things to Do in Istanbul: Top Sights and Neighbourhoods

Travel · Routevia · 5 min read · Updated June 2026

Unveiling the Magic of the City on Two Continents

Straddling Europe and Asia across the glittering Bosphorus Strait, Istanbul is a metropolis where layers of history peel back to reveal a vibrant, modern culture. For centuries, it served as the capital of the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires, leaving behind a legacy of architectural wonders, culinary traditions, and diverse communities. If you are planning a trip, finding the best things to do in Istanbul requires balancing world-famous historical monuments with the local rhythms of its distinct neighbourhoods.

The Historic Heart: Essential Things to Do in Sultanahmet

No first-time visit is complete without exploring Sultanahmet, the historic peninsula where Istanbul’s most iconic landmarks stand side by side. This compact area allows you to walk through centuries of Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman history in a single afternoon.

The Architectural Giants

Start your journey at the breathtaking Hagia Sophia, a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture that has stood for nearly 1,500 years. Originally built as a cathedral, it later became a mosque, a museum, and is now an active mosque once again. Just across the manicured square sits the Sultan Ahmed Mosque, universally known as the Blue Mosque due to the tens of thousands of hand-painted blue Iznik tiles adorning its interior walls.

Subterranean Wonders and Imperial Palaces

A short walk leads you to the Basilica Cistern, an ancient underground water reservoir supported by 336 marble columns, including the famous carved Medusa heads. To experience the opulence of the Ottoman sultans, spend a few hours wandering through the pavilions, courtyards, and sacred relic rooms of Topkapi Palace, which offers panoramic views of the Golden Horn.

Exploring the Historic Neighbourhoods of the European Side

Beyond the ancient walls of Sultanahmet lies a collection of diverse neighbourhoods, each boasting its own distinct personality and charm.

Galata and Karakoy: Historic Charm Meets Trendy Vibes

Cross the Galata Bridge—where local anglers cast their lines day and night—to reach Karakoy and Galata. The medieval Galata Tower dominates the skyline, offering 360-degree views of the city. The surrounding cobblestone streets are lined with independent boutiques, local designer shops, and hip cafes.

Navigating the Steep Streets of Galata

The geography of this area is famously hilly. Wear comfortable shoes as you climb the historic Kamondo Stairs, a beautiful neo-Baroque winding staircase built in the 1870s, which connects the bustling seaside of Karakoy to the cultural hub of Galata.

Fener and Balat: A Kaleidoscope of Colour

Located along the banks of the Golden Horn, the historic Greek and Jewish quarters of Fener and Balat are famous for their pastel-coloured wooden houses, steep streets, and bohemian atmosphere. It is the perfect area to get lost with a camera, browse antique shops, and visit the majestic red-brick Phanar Greek Orthodox College.

Crossing to Asia: The Vibrant Spirit of Kadikoy

To truly understand Istanbul, you must leave Europe behind. Boarding a public ferry to Kadikoy on the Asian side is one of the most scenic and affordable things to do in Istanbul.

The Bustling Kadikoy Market

Kadikoy is the cultural heartbeat of the city's youth. The daily market is a sensory overload of fresh fish, colorful spices, pickled vegetables, and local cheeses. It is also home to some of the city's best casual eateries, where you can sample traditional home-cooked Turkish dishes like lahmacun (thin, crispy Turkish pizza) and meze.

Moda: Seaside Parks and Creative Hubs

Adjacent to Kadikoy is Moda, a leafy residential neighbourhood. Here, locals gather on the grassy coastal parks to watch the sunset over the Sea of Marmara. The streets are filled with third-wave coffee shops, vinyl stores, and art galleries, offering a relaxed contrast to the bustling historic centre.

How to Plan Your Perfect Istanbul Itinerary

With so many sights spread across two continents, planning a cohesive route can feel overwhelming. To make the most of your time, it is best to group your activities by geographical proximity.

Maximising Your Days

Dedicate one full day to the historic sights of Sultanahmet, another day to the cultural corridor of Istiklal Street, Galata, and Karakoy, and a third day to exploring the Asian side of Kadikoy. To help navigate these distinct zones seamlessly, digital tools can be incredibly useful. While paper maps have their charm, modern travellers often rely on the Go Türkiye official tourism portal for up-to-date museum hours, alongside smart travel apps that use AI to calculate the most efficient walking routes and transit connections between neighbourhoods.

Embrace the Rhythm of Istanbul

Whether you are marveling at the golden mosaics of the Hagia Sophia, sipping Turkish tea on a Bosphorus ferry, or getting lost in the colourful labyrinth of Balat, Istanbul promises an unforgettable sensory journey. By balancing the must-see historic monuments with deep dives into local neighbourhoods, you will experience the true, multifaceted soul of this historic metropolis.
