How to plan a Türkiye road trip: a step-by-step itinerary guide

Travel · 7 min read · Updated June 2026

Türkiye is one of the most rewarding countries in the world to explore by car (the official Go Türkiye tourism board is a great starting point): ancient ruins, a turquoise coastline, mountain villages and food that changes from city to city. But that same variety is what makes planning a Türkiye road trip feel overwhelming. This step-by-step guide gives you a simple framework — how to choose your route, which regions to pick, how long to stay in each city, and how to map the whole itinerary in minutes.

Choosing your Türkiye road trip route

The easiest way to start a Türkiye road trip is to fix one point and choose a direction, rather than trying to plan every stop at once.

Start with your arrival city

Most road trips begin where you fly in — usually İstanbul, İzmir or Antalya. Use that city as your anchor and build outward, so you are never backtracking across the country to catch a flight home.

Pick a direction, not a full itinerary

Choose a single corridor: the Aegean coast heading south from İzmir, the Mediterranean "Turquoise Coast" around Antalya, or the Cappadocia and Central Anatolia loop. Each makes a natural one-week Türkiye road trip without endless driving.

How long to spend in each city

A relaxed pace beats cramming. A reliable rhythm for a Türkiye road trip is "3-2-1": three nights in your main hub, two nights in a second city, and one-night stops in between. This avoids the classic mistake of changing hotels every single day and spending the whole trip packing and driving instead of exploring.

The best regions for a Türkiye road trip

Instead of chasing a scattered wishlist of famous sights, pick one region and go deep. You will see more, drive less, and discover the small towns that become the highlight of the trip.

The Aegean coast

From İzmir south through Çeşme, Ephesus, Şirince and Bodrum — Greek and Roman history, vineyards and beach towns in easy driving distance of each other.

The Mediterranean Turquoise Coast

Around Antalya, Fethiye, Kaş and Olympos — dramatic coastline, ancient Lycian ruins and some of the best swimming in the country.

Cappadocia and Central Anatolia

Fairy chimneys, cave hotels, hot-air balloons and underground cities — a completely different landscape that pairs well with a stop in Konya or Ankara.

Mapping your Türkiye road trip the easy way

Once you have a region and a rough rhythm, mapping the route is the final step. List your cities in order, estimate driving time between each, and check that no single day involves more than three or four hours behind the wheel. Leaving a little slack in the schedule is what makes a road trip feel like a holiday rather than a logistics exercise.

Packing for a Türkiye road trip

Even by car, packing light pays off — there is less to load and unload at every stop. A short, weather-aware packing list keeps you mobile across Türkiye's surprisingly varied climate, from the hot coast to the cool highlands. Plan roughly 70% of your days and leave 30% open: the best Türkiye road trip moments — a roadside gözleme stand, a viewpoint you stumble on, a town that invites you to stay another night — are rarely on the itinerary.

Choose a region, set a gentle rhythm, map it fast, and let the country fill in the rest.
