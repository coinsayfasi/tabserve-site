First-time landlord? 7 things to track from day one

Property · 6 min read

Becoming a landlord is mostly about good record-keeping. The owners who stay calm at tax time and never lose a deposit dispute aren't lucky — they simply tracked the right things from the start. Here are the seven that matter most.

1. Rent payments

Log every payment: date received, amount, and which month it covers. A clear payment history protects you if a tenant ever disputes whether they paid, and makes spotting a late or partial payment instant.

2. The security deposit

Record the amount, the date received, and where it's held. Many regions require deposits to be protected in a specific scheme and returned within a set window — get this wrong and you can owe penalties.

3. The lease and key dates

Keep the signed lease plus its start date, end date, and renewal or notice deadlines. Knowing exactly when a fixed term ends — and when notice is due — prevents accidental rollovers and awkward conversations.

4. Expenses (every single one)

Repairs, insurance, management fees, mortgage interest, travel to the property — these are often tax-deductible, but only if you have the records. Save receipts as you go; reconstructing a year of expenses in April is misery.

5. Maintenance requests

Track what was reported, when, and how it was resolved. A documented maintenance trail keeps the property in good shape and is strong evidence you met your obligations as a landlord.

6. Your numbers (yield & cash flow)

Know your rental yield and monthly cash flow from day one. These two figures tell you whether the property is actually performing — and whether a future rent change or refinance makes sense. A quick calculator beats guessing.

7. Rent-increase rules

Before raising rent, check what's legal where the property is. Many places cap how much and how often you can increase, and require written notice. Getting this right keeps the increase enforceable and the relationship healthy.

Track these seven from the first month and being a landlord becomes calm, organized and genuinely profitable.
